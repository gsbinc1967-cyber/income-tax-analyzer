# Landmark Case Laws & Disputable Areas

## 1. Joint Development Agreements (JDA)
**Dispute:** When does the "transfer" happen in a JDA for capital gains tax?
- **Chaturbhuj Dwarkadas vs. CIT (SC)**: Merely entering into a JDA and granting a license to enter the property does not constitute 'possession' under Section 53A of Transfer of Property Act. Capital gains are not triggered just by signing the JDA.
- **Seshasayee Steels Pvt. Ltd. (SC)**: Confirmed that "transfer" under Section 2(47)(v) happens when actual physical possession coupled with control is handed over to the developer, allowing them to construct and sell.
- **Section 45(5A) [ITA 1961]**: Introduced to ease the burden on individuals/HUFs entering JDAs. Capital gains arise only in the year the certificate of completion for the whole or part of the project is issued.

## 2. Real Estate Sector
- **ALV of Unsold Inventory**: Income Tax Act historically sought to tax the Annual Let-out Value (ALV) of unsold flats held by developers. The amendment now exempts ALV for 2 years from the end of the financial year in which the completion certificate is received.
- **Subsidies / Grants**: **CIT vs. Ponni Sugars (SC)**: The character of a subsidy (capital vs revenue) depends on the *purpose* for which the subsidy is given, not the source or form.

## 3. IT & Software Sector
- **Engineering Analysis Centre of Excellence (SC)**: Payments made by resident Indian end-users/distributors to non-resident computer software manufacturers for the resale/use of computer software do not amount to "Royalty" under DTAA. Therefore, no TDS is required.

## 4. Reassessment (Sec 147 / 148)
- **GKN Driveshafts (India) Ltd. (SC)**: Established the procedure for reopening assessments. Once the assessee files the return in response to a 148 notice, they can demand the "reasons recorded" for reopening. The Assessing Officer (AO) must provide them, and the assessee can file objections which the AO must dispose of via a speaking order before proceeding with the assessment.
- **CIT vs. Kelvinator of India Ltd (SC)**: Reopening of assessment cannot be done merely on a "change of opinion" by the AO if the facts were fully disclosed during the original assessment under 143(3).

## 5. Penalty (Sec 270A / 271)
- **CIT vs. Reliance Petroproducts (SC)**: Making an incorrect claim in law does not automatically amount to "concealment of income" or "furnishing inaccurate particulars" if all facts material to the computation of income have been disclosed.
