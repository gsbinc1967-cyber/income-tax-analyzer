# Tax Planning Guide

Tax planning involves legitimately optimizing your tax liabilities using exemptions, deductions, and structural arrangements within the framework of the Income Tax Act.

## 1. Salary Restructuring
- **Opt for Allowances with Exemptions**: Structure the CTC to include HRA (House Rent Allowance), LTA (Leave Travel Allowance), and Food Coupons (up to ₹50/meal) instead of a high Basic Salary.
- **NPS Corporate Model (Sec 80CCD(2))**: Employer contributions to NPS (up to 10% of Basic + DA) are fully exempt in the hands of the employee and deductible for the employer. This is over and above the ₹1.5L (80C) and ₹50k (80CCD(1B)) limits.

## 2. Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)
- **Separate Tax Entity**: An HUF is treated as a separate entity for tax purposes. By transferring ancestral property or generating income through an HUF, you get an additional basic exemption limit and a separate set of 80C deductions.
- **Caution**: Clubbing provisions apply if personal funds are transferred to the HUF without adequate consideration.

## 3. Capital Gains Planning
- **Tax Harvesting (Equities)**: Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) on equity shares/equity MFs are exempt up to ₹1 Lakh (or ₹1.25 Lakh as per new budget proposals) per financial year. You can sell and immediately buy back shares to "harvest" this exemption every year, resetting your cost base.
- **Section 54/54F/54EC**:
  - **Sec 54/54F**: Reinvest capital gains from property (54) or any other asset (54F) into a new residential house to claim exemption.
  - **Sec 54EC**: Invest capital gains from land/building into specified bonds (NHAI, REC) up to ₹50 Lakhs within 6 months to save tax.
- **Indexation Benefit (Long‑Term Capital Gains on Immovable Property)**: Apply the Cost Inflation Index (CII) to compute the indexed cost of acquisition; reduces taxable gain.
- **Set‑Off Rules**:
  - **Short‑Term Capital Loss (STCL)** can be set off against both STCG and LTCG.
  - **Long‑Term Capital Loss (LTCL)** can be set off **only** against LTCG.
  - **Unabsorbed Loss Carry‑Forward**: Up to 8 years for capital losses.
- **Capital Gains Exemptions for Specific Assets**:
  - **Section 54F** for any asset (e.g., land, shares) reinvested in a residential house.
  - **Section 54B** for agricultural land transferred to a family member.
  - **Section 54D** for units of an equity‑linked savings scheme (ELSS) transferred.
- **Planning for Property Sales**:
  - Split a large transaction into multiple financial years to stay within exemption limits.
  - Use the “roll‑over” of gains into a new property to defer tax.

## 4. Setting Off and Carry Forward of Losses
- **Short Term Capital Loss (STCL)** can be set off against both STCG and LTCG.
- **Long Term Capital Loss (LTCL)** can ONLY be set off against LTCG.
- **House Property Loss** can be set off against any other head of income in the same year up to ₹2 Lakhs. Unabsorbed loss can be carried forward for 8 years but can only be set off against House Property income.

## 5. Regime Selection
- **New Regime vs Old Regime**: Calculate taxes under both. The New Regime offers lower slab rates but removes major deductions (HRA, LTA, 80C, 80D, Home Loan interest on self‑occupied property). Salaried individuals with high investments/home loans often benefit more from the Old Regime.

## 6. Investments under Section 80C & 80CCD
- **ELSS Mutual Funds**: Up to ₹1.5 Lakh deduction, lock‑in period 3 years, provide equity exposure.
- **Public Provident Fund (PPF)**: 15‑year lock‑in, deduction up to ₹1.5 Lakh, interest tax‑free.
- **Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana**: For girl child, deduction up to ₹1.5 Lakh, attractive interest rate.
- **National Pension Scheme (NPS) – Employee (80CCD(1))**: Additional ₹50 k deduction over 80C limit (total ₹2 Lakh).
- **Life Insurance Premium (80C)**: Up to ₹1.5 Lakh combined with other 80C instruments.

## 7. Health Insurance (Section 80D)
- **Self & Family**: Up to ₹25 k for < 60 yr, ₹50 k for senior citizen (including parents).
- **Preventive Health Check‑up**: Additional ₹5 k within the above limits.

## 8. Home Loan Interest (Section 24(b))
- **Self‑Occupied Property**: Deduction up to ₹2 Lakh on interest paid.
- **Let‑Out Property**: Entire interest deductible, subject to loss set‑off limits.

## 9. Education Loan (Section 80E)
- **Interest on Education Loan**: Deduction for up to 8 years, no upper limit, for higher education of self, spouse, children, or student sibling.

## 10. Donations (Section 80G)
- **Qualified Charitable Institutions**: 100 % deduction without limit for certain NGOs, 50 % deduction up to 10 % of gross total income for others.

## 11. Agricultural Income
- **Exemption Threshold**: Agricultural income up to ₹5 Lakh is exempt; above this, it is aggregated with total income for rate‑slab calculation but tax is paid only on the excess portion.

## 12. Presumptive Taxation for Business (Section 44AD/44AE)
- **Section 44AD**: For businesses with turnover ≤ ₹2 Crore, taxable income deemed at 6 % (or 8 % for digital receipts) of gross receipts.
- **Section 44AE**: For transport operators with ≤ 10 vehicles, deemed income is ₹40,000 per vehicle.
- **Benefit**: Simplified filing, no detailed books required, but cannot claim certain deductions.

## 13. Other Deductions
- **Section 80EEA**: Additional home loan interest deduction up to ₹1.5 Lakh for first‑time home buyers (subject to conditions).
- **Section 80GG**: Rent paid deduction for those not receiving HRA, subject to conditions.
