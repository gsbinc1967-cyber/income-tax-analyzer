# Notice & Assessment Handling Guide

A guide on how to respond to common notices issued by the Income Tax Department.

## 1. Intimation u/s 143(1)
- **What it is**: An automated processing message indicating whether your tax calculation matches the department's calculation. It may result in a refund, no demand, or a tax demand.
- **Action**: Check if the demand is due to a mismatch in TDS (Form 26AS/AIS) or a clerical error. You can file a Rectification Request u/s 154 if there is an apparent mistake, or simply pay the demand if it is correct.

## 2. Defective Return Notice u/s 139(9)
- **What it is**: The return filed is considered defective (e.g., missing audit report, unpaid taxes, incorrect ITR form).
- **Action**: You have **15 days** to rectify the defect from the date of receiving the notice. Log in to the e-Filing portal -> e-File -> Response to Notice u/s 139(9) -> Submit a revised XML/JSON correcting the error.

## 3. Limited / Complete Scrutiny Notice u/s 143(2)
- **What it is**: The case is picked up for scrutiny assessment. Time limit to issue this notice is 3 months from the end of the financial year in which the return was filed.
- **Action**:
  1. Check jurisdiction and validity (date of issue).
  2. Notice is now handled via the Faceless Assessment scheme.
  3. You will receive a questionnaire u/s 142(1) asking for specific documents (bank statements, proofs of deductions, loan confirmations).
  4. Submit all replies and PDFs strictly through the e-Filing portal within the stipulated time. Seek adjournments if more time is needed.

## 4. Reassessment Notice u/s 148 / 148A
- **What it is**: Issued if the AO has "information suggesting that income has escaped assessment".
- **Action**: 
  1. Under the new regime (Sec 148A), the AO must first issue a show-cause notice (148A(b)) and consider your reply before issuing the actual 148 notice (unless it's a search/seizure case).
  2. If a 148 notice is issued, you must file a fresh Income Tax Return within the time given.
  3. Request the "reasons recorded" for reopening (as per GKN Driveshafts ruling) and file objections if the reopening is based on a mere change of opinion or lacks tangible material.

## 5. Notice for Information u/s 133(6)
- **What it is**: The department is asking for information regarding your transactions or someone else's transactions with you.
- **Action**: Provide the factual information requested (e.g., confirmation of ledger accounts, copies of invoices). It does not mean you are under scrutiny, but non-compliance can attract a penalty under section 272A.
